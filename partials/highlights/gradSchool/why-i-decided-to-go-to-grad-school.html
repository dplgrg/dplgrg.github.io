Why I decided to go to graduate school

June 11, 2018 Graduate School

Back in the winter of 2016, I decided I didn't want to stick to the status quo. I'd landed my dream job, but something was amiss. I soon realized that despite having an undergraduate degree from a top institution in India, something was wanting in my education and I felt the need to upgrade my skills.

There was a lot of talk about Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning around me, but it turns out that most of it was just chatter. I finished Andrew Ng's course but felt the need to get a more comprehensive and hands-on course. I looked up online about Machine Learning and Data Science and came to the conclusion that a Master's degree from US would equip me with the best possible abilites to excel in this field. There is a sharp learning curve involved in educating oneself in Data science and Machine learning and the challenge thus presented enticed me to getting a graduate education.

In India, a lot of startups are focusing on great work in the field of Machine Learning as there is a lot of potential for growth owing to the copious amount of data produced by the ever-increasing population. There is a dearth of qualified Data scientists in India. A degree from a great institute from US in this field would give anyone an advantage in the high-paying industry of Data science in India.

Thus I decided to go for a Masters degree to learn one of the most complex disciplines in the field of Computer Science and also to give a boost to my future career prospects in India.
